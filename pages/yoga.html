Yoga

Yoga has been a big enrichment of my life for the past 3 years. When I started, I was so immobile and not at all flexible. It was very frustrating seeing all the other people doing poses etc. when I wasn't even able to touch my toes.

Now I understand that Yoga is not at all about being flexible, being stretchy and strong. It is about doing something for your health and well-being, listening to your body and accepting your limitations without pushing or expecting your body to do things it just can't.

What most people understand as doing yoga, the physical aspect of it - called Asana, is just one small part of yoga.

There are 8 limbs of yoga (called the Ashtanga Yoga System) which are the following:

  1. Yama - self-behavior
    • Ahimsa: non-violence
    • Satya: truthfulness
    • Asteya: non-stealing
    • Brahmacharya: abstinence
    • Aparigraha: non-grasping

  2. Niyama - observances
    • Saucha: cleanliness
    • Santosha: contentment (with what already is)
    • Tapas: asceticism/discipline
    • Svadyaya: self-study
    • Isvara Pranidhana: surrendering to a higher power

  3. Asana - physical (body) postures
  4. Pranayama - breathing exercises
  5. Pratyahara - withdrawal of the senses
  6. Dharana - concentration
  7. Dhyana - meditation
  8. Samadhi - enlightenment
